YOUNOTE

People love Coursera for its free high quality online education, and Coursera's students, no matter what age, are dedicated learners. However, I noticed that when I (and others as well) tried to take notes on the online lectures in Coursera, problems came up. How can online learners learn while taking notes?

YouNote is a redesign of Coursera's note-taking experience with online education.

The motivation of this redesign comes from users who use Coursera and take notes with their computers/laptops, who presented a need for easier and faster control of both video and note-taking, without disrupting the note-taking and lecture-watching processes.


THE PROCESS

User Research, Ideation, Contextual Interviews, Heuristic Evaluation, Wireframing, Prototyping, Iterative Design

In general, users had constraints of slow movement when switching screens and attempting to control each application, whether it was the video or the place where they took notes. The problem often arose because users often faced the constraints of a small screen. During research, one of the most interesting things was how diverse the Coursera community was, and how each of them were eager learners. While experts didn't write much notes and often played the video at a 1.5x speed, novices needed to write down a lot of notes and paused the video a lot to jot down what the professor said. Slow movement was often due to moving to the mouse to control another application, and not the user's ability to write notes, which is why the new design includes keyboard controls.
