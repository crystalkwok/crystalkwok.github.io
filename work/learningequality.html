Learning Equality is a non-profit that seeks to enable universal access to high quality open educational resources, and to create tools that empower communities to author and share their own content. Learning Equality is committed to enabling every person in the world to achieve a quality basic education, through supporting the creation and distribution of open educational resources, and facilitating their use inside and outside of classrooms around the world. They have created KA Lite, which is an open-source platform for viewing and interacting with Khan Academy videos and exercises, can be used even when no Internet is available. Students can connect to the server from within the classroom, on a laptop, tablet, or desktop computer, or it can be installed directly on a learner's computer for portable access. I am currently interning here as a user experience design intern, and am in the process of designing the experience and interface of the content curation process, where teachers can add and share their own content to teach their students on this platform.

Our team narrowed down to four use case scenarios, which includes:


  • creating own content, independent of Khan Academy (KA) or anyone else
  • adding their own content to existing KA content, remixing according to curriculum
  • remixing KA content, which adding their own content
  • the content curator sends massive amounts of content to Learning Equality to organize, import, and create a topic tree

We also created more specific use case scenarios based on the features we would have, and covering various cases (especially figuring out the corner cases for users!) We then conducted user research on Noemi from War Child Holland, who currently uses Learning Equality as a platform to host her content, but has no interface to curate her own content directly. Through our user research, we also found the distinction between :


  • content driven curation: the user usually dumps all their content into the platform, then organizes it gradually through the whole process, often changing where it goes
  • topic driven curation: the user already has in mind the types of topics they want, and exactly where the content would go. The user usually organizes and creates the topics before they put in the content.

Although we agreed that topic driven makes the process of curating content simpler, easier, and cleaner, Noemi mentioned that the topics she makes and where she places her content was flexible, because she had no set place for them in the first place and was unsure of how everything would fit together until she started putting them into topic trees. And because of this, she often moved her content around a lot. So we decided to make the platform able to be catered to the content-driven curation style, as well as the topic-driven curation style.
