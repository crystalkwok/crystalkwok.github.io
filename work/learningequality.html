LEARNING EQUALITY

Learning Equality is a non-profit that seeks to enable universal access to high quality open educational resources, and to create tools that empower communities to author and share their own content. Learning Equality is committed to enabling every person in the world to achieve a quality basic education, through supporting the creation and distribution of open educational resources, and facilitating their use inside and outside of classrooms around the world. I am currently interning here as a user experience design intern, and am in the process of designing the experience and interface of the content curation process, where teachers can add and share their own content to teach their students on this platform.

We conducted user research on Noemi from War Child Holland, who currently uses Learning Equality as a platform to host her content, but has no interface to curate her own content directly. Through our user research, we also found the distinction between :


  • content driven curation: the user usually dumps all their content into the platform, then organizes it gradually through the whole process, often changing where it goes
  • topic driven curation: the user already has in mind the types of topics they want, and exactly where the content would go. The user usually organizes and creates the topics before they put in the content.

Although we agreed that topic driven makes the process of curating content simpler, easier, and cleaner, Noemi mentioned that the topics she makes and where she places her content was flexible, because she had no set place for them in the first place and was unsure of how everything would fit together until she started putting them into topic trees. And because of this, she often moved her content around a lot. So based on user research, we decided to make the platform able to be catered to the content-driven curation style, as well as the topic-driven curation style.

USER RESEARCH (part two)


Qualitative User Research


PARALLEL RESEARCH

The user starts with a bottom up method, by grabbing all the related content items related to one single item and grouping them together

The user ends up with a vertical hierarchy when she is done organizing things together, and clumps together everything that is related

For our 2nd iteration of user research, we used the talk-out-loud method with our third iteration prototype, and the parallel research method with different types of content we put together. The talk-out-loud method just included giving the prototype to our users and watching their pain points as they used the prototype. The parallel research method was the most interesting, because without any major influence from the designers, we gave them content to organize together, in order to watch how people usually organize content together. This method could give us a hint into the mental models a user has in doing an activity, and what they would naturally do and how they would think during the activity. For our parallel research method, we gave them slips of paper to organize into groups to see how people organize their content, which is what content curation is all about.


Here are some of our insights:
talk-out-loud method:

  • showing the end product always helps them with knowing how they should start building it
  • the wording of "topic" and "content" are unfamiliar to users-- possibly renaming to "folder" and "files"
  • a blank screen in the beginning is never great-- every user needs at least a first step guidance, or should include inline-help
  • take out redundancy-- it'll confuse the users

parallel research method:

  • users first separated by the overall idea first
  • the process was very bottom up-- users first thought of the general idea, took out the content, then separated them into even more smaller sections
  • users grouped content together first and then created topic names
  • users don't like a lot of levels-- only if there is a massive amount of content will they create more levels to organize
  • users usually created a vertical hierarchy to visualize their content

FUTURE DIRECTIONS


We will be making the appropriate changes from user research to the design, which includes:

  • first step guidance
  • renaming to fit the mental models in the users' heads
  • creating a workspace where people can dump their content (upload or drag and drop) their first in there, then drag their finalized groupings into the finalized topic tree. This is to fit the bottom-up process users usually use

We hope to be able to code up this part of the project and continue iterating on the design!
