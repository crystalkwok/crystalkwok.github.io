CULTIVATION PROJECT

easy connecting between missionaries and supporters

Missionaries are having trouble finding supporters and finding an easy way to keep them updated; supporters are finding it difficult to remember to read all the posts and prayer requests. The Cultivation Project hopes to connect supporters to missionaries in an easier way, and to encourage more prayer as the main avenue to support missionaries.

This project is still in it's beginning stages of completing its MVP, but I believe this will solve some pain points supporters and missionaries have been experiencing. The Cultivation Project had a booth at Chinese Missions Conference (CMC) and had great feedback from people there.

challenge

Barriers to Supporting and Being Supported

Missionaries often need more emotional support and want to share their stories, while supporters often face barriers to supporting them whole-heartedly. We also wanted to encourage a heart for missions and what's going on in the world. How can help this with Christians globally?

personal journey

Physical, Emotional, Spiritual Updates

All supporters emphasized wanting to know journey the missionary was on-- spiritually, emotionally, and physically. Unlike other general lifestyle blog posts that are single photos, quotes, or life advice that are generally separate, updates are usually more about sharing their thoughts, feelings, and what they learned throughout the trip. The individual parts together paint a better picture of the missionary, the people they are serving, and their current trip.

Separate Parts Make Up the Whole

Reflecting through design how users thought of updates as a part of a bigger journey, with the physical trip and the emotional and spiritual ups and downs of the missionary, makes accessing common actions much easily. Even though we cleared the clutter to focus the user as they read that particular update, they needed an easy way to understand the bigger picture and access other updates easily. Understanding that posts were highly connected translated to having an birds-eye overview of the missionary’s journey and easy access to other related posts from the trip. Each post is highlighted as a point in time through the vertical design, using time and journey as the key ways to understand the updates. Location tags showed the physical journey where the missionary travelled.

context

Forgetful and Behind

Supporters often mentioned while they had tried to follow some of their friends and missionaries, they often forgot the context. They may have missed a few posts along the way due to notifications about posts coming at inconvenient times, or the initial support letter was sent so long ago that they forgot what this team was doing on this trip. While supporters tended to go for the most recent update, understanding the story and context was key to even understanding the current blog post. If the supporter was reading the reflection, what had happened before?

Providing Context

Quick reminders are placed on each post where, after much research, supporters would often need reminders on what the missions trip was about. Time references (long term or a date range) gives hints to the users about what stage the missionary is at. We wanted the supporter to understand the context and bigger picture as quickly as possible, so they stayed invested in the missionary. Being lost and confused in the journey was a huge barrier. We found that because some updates were missed, or understanding the context was too much work loses the supporters' interest in keeping up with the missionary's journey.

culture

For Discovery and Personal Passion

Supporters usually had a group of people with a particular culture they had a heart for -- Japanese, Chinese, etc. While missionaries around the world face similar problems, serving a particular culture would provide its own set of struggles of how to effectively reach the people due to their culture. Supporters were more likely to venture out to find missionaries if they served the particular people group they were searching for, if the missionary's activity was something they were interested in for the future, or something they were currently doing. Also, when naming their trips, missionaries always included something about the location in the name.

Providing Location

Location here is emphasized more here because the information means a lot more in this context to the supporters. By displaying the location more prominently, locations and cultures that interest supporters can easily be noticed, discovered, and followed. We display it upfront in every section, especially in discovery, because of how crucial it was for people to understand the context of the update or in showing interest, as we found through research. The location helped in understanding the culture and people the missionary was serving and trying to enter into, as well as the struggles they often faced with that culture and location. Other pieces of information was considered, but was taken out because it didn’t hold the same weight as location.

improvements and takeaways

Focusing on Designing from the Research of our Users

Overall, I wish we could have done more preliminary user research before we started building. Hopefully, the released MVP will produce some good research points and design changes to improve the experience.
