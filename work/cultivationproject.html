CULTIVATION PROJECT

easy connecting between missionaries and supporters

Missionaries struggle to gather supporters and keep them updated; supporters have difficulty keeping up with all updates, and often stopping when they're overwhelmed when trying to catch up. The Cultivation Project hopes to connect supporters to missionaries easily, and to encourage more prayer as the main avenue to support missionaries.

Deliverables: Invision Link

Role:

UX/UI Designer

Collaborators:

Cameron Kuey, Ricky Nguyen, Josephine Sheu, Teresa Yuan, Matthew Woo, Gensen Choy, Tony Zhang

challenge

Barriers to Supporting and Being Supported

Missionaries hope to share their stories and gather emotional support, yet have trouble sharing with existing supporters in an easy way (mailing lists, facebook events), and gathering new supporters. Supporters struggle to support them whole-heartedly, due to the inability to keep up with their missionary consistently, yet feel overwhelmed and unmotivated when there's too much already missed.

From our research, the biggest pain point was effective mass communication on both sides, so we wanted to focus on solving that issue.

For everything, we also explored multiple options for profiles and discovery pages to address different issues (ex: wireframe below).

personal journey

Physical, Emotional, Spiritual Updates

All supporters emphasized understanding the missionary's journey-- spiritually, emotionally, and physically. Other general lifestyle blog posts feature unconnected single photos, quotes, or life advice; however, shared thoughts, feelings, and what was learned throughout the trip links updates together to paint a better picture of the missionary, the people being served, and the overall journey.

Separate Parts Make Up the Whole

Users often read updates one after the other, and wanted to quickly see how it progressed from the beginning. We focused on the written content of the post until intentional interaction to view other posts.

By creating easy access to a list of posts chronologically, common actions became more accessible, and recognizes each post as part of a bigger journey. A bird's eye overview of all the posts helps supporters quickly understand the journey, how this post relates to all other posts, and access other posts. Each post is highlighted as a particular point in time and location in the journey through the vertical design. Location tags hint at the physical journey of the missionary.

context

Forgetful and Behind

While supporters tried following some of their friends and missionaries, they often forgot the context when a new update came. They may have missed a few posts along the way due to notifications coming at inconvenient times, or the initial support letter was sent so long ago that they forgot what was happening this trip. Supporters tended to go for the most recent update, but knowing the story and context was key to even understanding the current post. If the supporter was reading the reflection, what had happened before?

Providing Context

Being lost, confused, and overwhelmed from keeping up with the journey was a huge barrier. By providing reminders on context and the bigger picture, we hope to increase long-term investment into the missionary, so supporters can easily catch up.

After much research in what context supporters often need reminders, quick mission summaries are placed on each post where. Time references (long term or a date range) give hints to the supporters about what stage the missionary is at. By providing clarity immediately and easily, we give supporters a sense of comfort to avoid feeling overwhelmed.

culture

For Discovery and Personal Passion

Supporters generally support a group they were passionate about with a particular culture -- Japanese, Chinese, etc. While missionaries around the world face similar problems, serving a particular culture provided its own set of struggles on how to effectively cross those cultural barriers.

Supporters were more likely to venture out and find missionaries if they served the particular people group they were searching for, if the missionary's activity was something they were interested in or currently doing. Also, when naming their trips, missionaries always included something about the location in the name.

Providing Location

By displaying the location more prominently, locations and cultures that interest supporters can easily be noticed, discovered, then followed. We display it upfront in every section, especially in discovery, to quickly understand the context, and to interest those searching for missionaries to support. The location helped in understanding the journey the missionary was on, such as knowing the struggles they often faced with that particular culture and people.

improvements and takeaways

Focusing on Designing from the Research of our Users

Overall, I wish we could have done more preliminary user research before we started building. Hopefully, the released MVP will produce some good research points and design changes to improve the experience.
