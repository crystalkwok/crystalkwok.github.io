TIMELINE

In the traditional calendar view, our cognitive efforts are expended equally across both a job interview and a psychology class, even though we need to spend more time and attention preparing for the job interview. How can we see what events are more important and take higher priority in our lives?

Timeline is a working mobile application that visualizes people's personal events better based on priority.

We believe that Timeline will help users manage their time better and make better decisions about what to pay attention to next by quickly knowing the priority of their events. As a better visualizer of the importance of events, the Timeline serves as quicker and better reminders of the important events in users' lives.

This was a great way to further explore the subject of time and information visualization in the Cognitive Science realm, and a great way to see what goes into designing the tiny details of a mobile app that often do not get thought of in a short term project. In the end, we presented our final presentation to Don Norman, Jim Hollan, and Scott Klemmer, and we got feedback on what each of them thought of this new idea.

DESIGN DECISIONS

COLORS
"With a blue background, I can see the outlines, but it's not that obvious to me. I would prefer more contrast and a white background"
Colors indicate different psychological effects, so we wondered, "Could color indicate importance and priority, especially with red hinting at a sense of urgency of upcoming events and decisions to be made?" We found that color made no significant difference, so we decided on a cleaner and simpler interface to make it easier for people to see their events and time.

SIZE
"The bigger the size, the more clearly it pops out to me"
We chose SIZE as our biggest indicator of priority and importance, because conveying the most important events as the first thing seen can subconsciously indicate to the user that this event is most important, and needs more attention, time, and decision-making to surround this event. Studies have also shown that it is quicker to detect things that are larger.

FIXED VS. RELATIVE TIME
FIXED: set amount of space determined by duration
pros:

  • every hour shown
  • visualization of length would be accurately representative of the duration and time in between ...  

cons:

  • huge chunks of empty space when no events
  • start at a set time, even if there were no events around the time
  • leaves screens empty without any information from calendar

RELATIVE: every hour and even may not be represented by a set amount of space, not every hour shown (more event based vs. hour based)
pros:

  • no empty screens
  • based on events, not the hour it is
MITIGATION:
  • when there were events: FIXED time to accurately visualize duration of events and time in between
  • 8 hrs+ without events: empty time is skipped and indicated with a vertical zigzag, to prevent from having any empty screens

END TIME
With the end point, we saw the difference between timeline and the traditional calendar view, where the calendar view may be better for planning for exact times, while the timeline is better for "general gists" of events and their importance. ...   With the end time, the timeline looked cluttered on the small screen. Although we agree that having an end time is the most ideal, we decided to not put the end time if the screen was too small. Duration of the event would be indicated by a colored tailed extending from the circle of the event, which would only give a gist of how long the event is based on how long the tail extended. However, if the screen of the device the user was using was big enough, we would put the end time in.

FEATURES


  • priority will be indicated by the size of the circle and the size of the text.

  • clean and simple interface with only 2 colors.

  • duration of an event will be indicated by the middle of a circle with an extended tail, along with the start time (and possibly end time, depending on the screen size).

  • current time will be displayed by a red line.

  • combination between relative and fixed time ::
    • max 5 events on the screen at at time.
    • days will start with the first event.
    • time breaks of 8 hours or more will be indicated by a zigzag line or a block with the date if it is a new day.
