MUSTASKE

Based on user research from Flunkless, we saw the need for synchronous communication between professors and students during lecture, because students were often too scared to ask questions during lecture and felt like technology often gave them the courage to ask their questions because virtual communication is more comfortable for students. How can we facilitate communication during lecture between many students to one professor?

MustAske is a live chat application that uses voting of questions and polls to facilitate in class lectures.

Our other motivating factors shown from user research were that students often felt judged by their peers and their professors, so we felt that students still needed a voice regardless of this fear. Also, how can the professor know which questions to answer in a sea of so many questions from so many students? We attempted to address these needs and concerns with this new application. The focus on this project was on software engineering, but we believe that all are needed-- design, software, and research.
