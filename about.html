Hello there! My name is Crystal Kwok. I'm currently a Cognitive Psychology major, Computer Science minor student at the University of California, San Diego. I stumbled upon the world of HCI/UX and instantly fell in love, so I'm currently taking more Human-Computer Interaction classes along with Cognitive Psychology, Social Psychology, and Computer Science classes.

I fell in love with UX design because not only does it combine all my passions (people, research, design, and coding), but it is also revolutionary in solving problems for change through creative ideas based on user needs. Not only does design change to fit the different users, needs, and contexts in our ever changing world, but design also has the power to change how people think and engage with products and experiences. I design to redefine how people think about and emotionally engage with common experiences that occur in every day lives in order to make what the user is passionate about matter most-- calendars to fulfill goals, offline education to empower human flourishing, and so much more. My education and research in Psychology helps me focus on users and understand the motivations that drive them. My current passion is on information visualization, and I am currently interning at Learning Equality and designing the content curation experience. I am also currently a research assistant in the Design Lab with Cat Hicks and Vineet Pandey, and the lab is headed by Don Norman, Scott Klemmer, and Jim Hollan.

When I am not working on my HCI projects, I am crafting, dancing, cooking and baking, watching Masterchef Australia, obsessing over typography, and serving with my church. :)

Currently located in:       Los Angeles and San Diego
