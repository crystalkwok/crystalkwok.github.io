CROWN

rule your life


DURATION: August 2014 - September 2014

ROLE: UX / UI Designer

TOOLS: Contextual Inquiry, Ethnographic Studies, Adobe Creative Suite, Front End Developing (HTML, CSS, JS/JQuery), Competitive Analysis, Wireframing, Prototyping, Work Flow Modelling

DESCRIPTION: goal-based calendar with a new way to visually interpret time


COLLABORATORS: Kaitlin Garriott, Nick Gibson, Michael Lee, Melinda Chu



Students often struggle with being able to manage all different aspects of their time, while remembering all the different events and when to start studying for exams. When will a full-time student find the time to fulfill their dream of becoming a doctor by applying to medical school while frantically trying to maintain their grades? Students also often struggle with wasting their free time on Facebook, thinking there is nothing they need to work on at that time, and thinking they have more time than they actually do (hence the massive amounts of cramming and procrastination). This is usually because students often lack the motivation to keep working hard in their studies, because they cannot see or even remember their end goal. Thus, our question is this: how can a calendar / planner help students be effective with their time. Studies have shown that people need to believe in what they do, which is why we have chosen to make Crown an interactive goal-based web calendar, where users can set their long-term and short-term goals. Our motivation for Crown is for students to effectively maximize their time to fulfill their goals, and essentially to make the important urgent.
