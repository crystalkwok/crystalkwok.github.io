People love Coursera for its free high quality education, and Coursera's students, no matter what age, are dedicated learners. However, I noticed that when I (and others as well) tried to take notes on the online lectures in Coursera, problems came up. My screen was too small to easily move between a PDF file and a video, and switching between screens and trying to catch (and missing) what the professor just said caused even more problems. That's why I decided to create YouNote, a redesign of the note-taking experience on Coursera for the KPBC Design Fellowship. I designed to optimize the note-taking experience by giving faster and easier control of the video and note-taking, without disrupting the note-taking process.

FINAL PROTOTYPE & DESIGN DECISIONS

The point of this redesign is because users who used Coursera and took notes with their computers/laptops presented a need for easier and faster control of both video and note-taking, without disrupting the note-taking process. Without YouNote, users had the constraints of slow movement due to using a mouse or trackpad to switch screens, then control each application, whether it was the video or the place where they took notes. Also, users often faced the constraints of a small screen, because most would watch on their laptops or computer screens. Therefore, the features and redesign reflects these user needs to give users of Coursera an easier experience with taking notes while watching a video lecture. A couple of features include combining both the video and the note-taking into one screen, having bigger buttons and captions, and using keyboard controls, like Ctrl-T for text, and Ctrl-P to pause/play the video. One of the challenges of this redesign was giving a lot of user control and freedom, such as being able to switch screens, because the users of Coursera are very diverse and have a lot of opinions and preferences on where everything should be, yet being able to maintain simplicity and usability for a faster experience.
