what do you do here?



My main roles at Rainbow Art & Rainbow Canvas are being an assistant art instructor to children and adults, and being the UX designer. Rainbow Art is a local art school for children, and Rainbow Canvas are various single classes where an art instructor will instruct step-by-step on the featured artwork of the night. I have taught colored pencil, watercolor, markers, and acrylic painting to various students (similar to Wine & Canvas, but without the wine).

I recently became the UX designer of Rainbow Art & Rainbow Canvas simply because I asked for more roles in practicing my Adobe Creative Suite skills, but also in helping to make the experience here more user-centered and to solve problems presented by the users. For example, one night during a Rainbow Canvas instruction, the original art instructor decided to instruct the students by showing the step-by-step painting on one side of the room, yet keeping the sample painting that she had finished earlier on the other side of the room. I noticed that the students kept straining their necks by turning back and forth to see the sample painting and the step-by-step painting, because they wished to continue to see the end result while seeing the steps. I notified the instructor of my observation and she moved the sample painting to the right of her step-by-step painting.

I have also made flyers, designed the Rainbow Canvas logo, started coding the company website, and made sample paintings for Rainbow Canvas.
