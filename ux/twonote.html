People love Coursera for its free high quality education, and Coursera's students are dedicated learners. However, I noticed that when I (and others as well) tried to take notes on the online lectures in Coursera, problems came up. My screen was too small to easily move between a PDF file and a video, and switching between screens and trying to catch (and missing) what the professor just said caused even more problems. That's why I decided to redesign the note-taking experience on Coursera for the KPBC Design Fellowship. My design optimizes the note-taking experience for online courses by giving the user an easy and intuitive experience.




FINAL PROTOTYPE & DESIGN DECISIONS

easy and faster control of both video and note-taking with keyboard controls, like Ctrl-T for text, and Ctrl-P to pause/play the video
easy control and changes of dual screen options, such as flipping screens
easy switching between dual and full screen
optimization for smaller screens due to the dual screen, like bigger captions and bigger buttons for the frequently used buttons
